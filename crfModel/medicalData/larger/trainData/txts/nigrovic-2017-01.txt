In Lyme disease–endemic areas, children frequently develop Borrelia infections after an unrecognized Ixodes scapularis tick bite.‍ [1] Common clinical manifestations include an erythema migrans (EM) lesion, facial palsy, meningitis, and arthritis. [‍2] However, Lyme “mimics” (ie, patients with clinical symptoms compatible with possible Lyme disease but who ultimately were found to have alternate diagnoses) cause initial diagnostic and therapeutic uncertainty.
